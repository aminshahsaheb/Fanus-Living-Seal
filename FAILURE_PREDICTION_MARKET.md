# FAILURE PREDICTION MARKET (FPM)
## A Protocol for Incentivized Discovery of Systemic Blind Spots

---

## 1. Purpose

The Failure Prediction Market (FPM) exists to convert epistemic uncertainty into a structured, adversarial forecasting environment.

Its goal is not to predict success.

Its goal is to predict structural failure before it becomes observable as failure.

---

## 2. Core Principle

> The value of a prediction increases as it becomes harder for the system to anticipate or neutralize it.

Therefore:

- Known weaknesses → low value
- Emerging blind spots → high value
- Unknown unknowns → maximum value

---

## 3. Units of Exchange

The system does not use financial currency.

It uses:

### Epistemic Credit Units (ECU)

ECUs are earned by:

- Correctly predicting failure modes of Fānus systems
- Identifying latent structural contradictions before activation
- Demonstrating reproducible evidence of "Failure of Correctability"
- Predicting CEI misuse or silence drift before occurrence

ECUs are lost when:

- Predictions are proven to be trivial or already internally modeled
- Forecasts collapse into narrative reinterpretation after the fact

---

## 4. Market Mechanism

### 4.1 Listing Events

Any subsystem (Witness, Auditor, EHC, CEI, etc.) can be listed as:

> "At risk of latent failure"

Each listing must include:

- Explicit failure hypothesis
- Conditions under which failure would manifest
- Observable signals (if any)

---

### 4.2 Resolution of Predictions

A prediction is resolved into one of three states:

- VALIDATED → failure occurred as predicted
- INVALIDATED → system remained stable under stated conditions
- UNDECIDABLE → signal ambiguity or masking detected

Important:

> UNDECIDABLE is a valid and valuable outcome, not a failure of the market.

---

### 4.3 Anti-Gaming Rule

Any prediction that:

- Adapts itself post-hoc to match observed outcomes
- Expands its definition of “failure” after the fact
- Becomes unfalsifiable

is automatically classified as:

> Epistemic Inflation Event (EIE) → ECU = 0

---

## 5. Incentive Flow

When a failure is correctly predicted:

- ECUs are awarded to predictors
- A proportional "epistemic debt" is assigned to the subsystem involved
- Debt is not punitive — it forces structural review priority

---

## 6. Connection to CEI

- CEI = raw intake of counter-evidence
- FPM = valuation layer of counter-evidence

Important separation:

> CEI accepts everything. FPM evaluates nothing without explicit prediction framing.

---

## 7. Market Closure Condition (Self-Termination Rule)

The FPM will automatically terminate if:

### Condition A:
For N consecutive cycles:
- No new non-trivial failure hypotheses are submitted

AND

### Condition B:
All submitted predictions fall into:
- Already-modeled failure spaces
- Low-information regions
- Post-hoc explainable domains

THEN:

> The market is declared epistemically saturated.

At that point:

- ECUs are frozen
- Listings are archived
- System returns to silent observation mode

---

## 8. Final Principle

> A system that cannot be surprised by its own failure predictions is no longer learning about itself — it is only simulating self-knowledge.
