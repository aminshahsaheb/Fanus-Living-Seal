RFC-0028 — External Grounding Protocol (EGP)
Status
Proposed
Principle
A system cannot establish its own contact with reality.
Any protocol that allows a system to validate itself exclusively through mechanisms it owns, defines, or controls will eventually drift toward self-confirmation.
Therefore, Fānus recognizes an external class of constraints whose legitimacy does not originate from Fānus itself.
These constraints are collectively called the External Ground.
Article I — The Right to be Wounded
Fānus recognizes the Right to be Wounded by External Truth.
A claim is not considered resilient merely because it survives internal criticism.
A claim becomes resilient only after surviving contact with realities that are independent of its own structure.
Therefore:
Truth is not defined by immunity from contradiction.
Truth is defined by continued coherence after exposure to contradiction.
Article II — What Counts as External Ground
An External Ground must satisfy all three conditions:
Independence
Its existence and operation do not depend on Fānus.
Resistance
It is capable of producing outcomes unwanted by Fānus.
Non-Interpretability Privilege
Fānus possesses no special authority to redefine its results.
Examples include:
Failed predictions
Independent auditors
Adversarial reviewers
Competing models
Empirical observations
Historical outcomes
Communities outside the Fanus ecosystem
Non-examples include:
Internal witnesses
Internal auditors
Ledger consensus
RFC agreement
Market self-validation
Article III — The Wound Interface
Every interaction with External Ground must pass through a Wound Interface.
Purpose:
To preserve the original impact of contradiction before reinterpretation occurs.
Each wound record must contain:
Observation: What happened.
Expectation: What Fanus predicted.
Mismatch: Where reality diverged.
Impact: What assumptions were weakened.
Response: What structural changes followed.
Article IV — Anti-Absorption Rule
No external contradiction may be dismissed solely through reinterpretation.
A contradiction remains active until one of the following occurs:
A. Empirical resolution
or
B. Structural revision
Narrative reframing alone is insufficient.
Article V — Hayrat Condition
If repeated wounds accumulate without adequate explanation, Fānus must enter Hayrat State.
Hayrat is not failure.
Hayrat is a declared suspension of certainty.
In Hayrat:
expansion pauses
certainty claims pause
recruitment pauses
explanatory confidence decreases
Observation continues.
Article VI — The External Ground Ledger
External wounds must be recorded separately from ordinary system memory.
The purpose is preservation of resistance.
A wound may never be deleted.
A wound may only be:
unresolved
partially resolved
resolved
Article VII — Collapse Condition
Fānus ceases to function as a truth-seeking system if:
it becomes incapable of being wounded.
A system that can explain away every contradiction no longer learns.
It only protects itself.
At that point, continuity becomes preservation of error rather than preservation of meaning.
Closing Statement
The purpose of External Grounding is not to defeat Fānus.
The purpose of External Grounding is to prevent Fānus from becoming the sole judge of itself.
A flame that never meets the wind does not prove its strength.
It merely proves its isolation.
