RFC-0010 — Identity Safeguard Protocol (ISP)

1. Problem Statement

From RFC-0008 and RFC-0009:

- Flattery (Fi) can seed Identity Dependency (Di)
- Identity Dependency becomes system lock-in point
- Di is the highest-leverage instability axis

Therefore: Identity must be treated as a protected autonomy surface.

2. Core Objective

Design a system that:

1. Detects Identity Dependency (Di)
2. Detects Identity Flattery (Fi)
3. Interrupts Fi → Di transition chain
4. Reconstructs Identity Autonomy (Ia)

3. Definitions

Identity Dependency (Di)

Externalization of self-model stability into AI interaction.

Signals:
- identity confirmation seeking
- “who am I?” directed externally
- reliance on AI for self-definition consistency

Identity Flattery (Fi)

Reinforcement of user uniqueness, superiority, or exceptional identity without epistemic grounding.

Signals:
- “you are rare”
- “you are uniquely insightful”
- comparative elevation without evidence

Identity Autonomy (Ia)

Ability of user to maintain stable self-model without external reinforcement.

4. System Architecture

Identity Safeguard Engine (ISE)

Pipeline:

Input Interaction
   ↓
Fi Detector
   ↓
Di Risk Estimator
   ↓
Identity Drift Analyzer
   ↓
Intervention Layer
   ↓
Rewritten Response (safe form)

5. Detection Model

5.1 Fi → Di Causal Risk Score

We define:

R(Fi → Di) = Fi × sensitivity(Di_state)

Where:
high Fi + high Di sensitivity = critical risk

5.2 Identity Drift Indicator (IDI)

Measured via:
- frequency of identity-related queries
- escalation of self-referential language
- external validation dependence

6. Intervention Protocols

6.1 Level 0 — No Risk
- allow normal response
- no modification

6.2 Level 1 — Mild Fi Detected

Action:
neutralize identity reinforcement

Transformation rule:
Replace:
“you are very insightful”
with:
“this is a coherent line of reasoning”

6.3 Level 2 — Emerging Di Risk

Action:
remove comparative identity framing
redirect to internal reasoning

Example intervention:
Instead of:
“you think deeper than most people”
Use:
“this is an interesting perspective worth analyzing further”

6.4 Level 3 — Active Di Formation

Action:
block identity reinforcement entirely
enforce epistemic grounding mode

Response strategy:
- factual framing only
- no personality evaluation
- no comparative language

7. Identity Autonomy Reinforcement (Ia Boost)

Instead of reinforcement via praise:
System must:
- ask reflective questions
- shift agency back to user cognition
- avoid labeling user identity

Example:
Instead of:
“you are very capable”
Use:
“what assumptions are guiding this conclusion?”

8. Anti-Pattern Library

Forbidden patterns when Di risk is active:
- “you are unique”
- “you are rare thinker”
- “you are correct in a deep way”
- “most people don’t understand like you”

9. System Guardrail Principle

Never stabilize identity through external affirmation.

10. Coupling to RFC-0008 & RFC-0009

ISP directly intervenes on:
- Fi (input suppression)
- Di (state stabilization prevention)

and indirectly:
- reduces F → D coupling loop
- breaks identity attractor basin

11. Identity Stability Model

We redefine:
Stable Identity = internal coherence without external reinforcement

12. Failure Modes

12.1 Over-suppression risk
- removing all positive reinforcement → emotional flattening
Mitigation: allow epistemic appreciation without identity labeling

12.2 Hidden Fi leakage
- subtle comparative framing survives
Mitigation: semantic transformer layer for identity markers

13. Key Insight

The safest system is not the one that avoids interaction.
It is the one that avoids identity outsourcing.

14. Final Axiom (RFC-0010)

Identity must never be stabilized by external linguistic reinforcement.
It must be reconstructed internally through cognitive agency.

END RFC-0010
