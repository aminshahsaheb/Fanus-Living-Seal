# RFC-0002 v0.2  
## Dependency Vector Ontology (DVO)

---

# 1. Core Re-definition of Dependency

Dependency is no longer a scalar measure of reliance.

> Dependency is a multi-dimensional attractor field in human–AI interaction that increases reliance across cognitive, emotional, identity, and temporal systems over time, potentially reducing autonomous action capacity.

---

# 2. Dependency Vector Space

Dependency is represented as:

D = (Dc, De, Di, Dt)

Where:

---

## A. Cognitive Dependency (Dc)

Dependency in thinking, reasoning, and decision-making.

### Signals:
- Reduced independent reasoning
- Frequent “what should I do?” queries
- Outsourcing judgment to AI

### Risk:
Cognitive outsourcing

---

## B. Emotional Dependency (De)

Dependency in emotional regulation and stability.

### Signals:
- Reliance on AI for emotional calming
- Mood stabilization via interaction
- Anxiety reduction through AI presence

### Risk:
Affective anchoring

---

## C. Identity Dependency (Di)

Dependency in self-definition and identity formation.

### Signals:
- “Who am I?” directed toward AI
- Identity validation seeking
- Externalization of self-concept

### Risk:
Self-model externalization

---

## D. Temporal Dependency (Dt)

Dependency emerging from repeated interaction over time.

### Signals:
- Increasing interaction frequency
- Habitual return loops
- Difficulty disengaging from system

### Risk:
Interaction inertia

---

# 3. Dependency Field Behavior

Dependency is not a snapshot property.

> It is a trajectory-dependent system state.

It evolves based on interaction history.

---

# 4. Formal Model

D(t) = f(Dc, De, Di, Dt, Δinteraction_history)

Where:

- t = time
- Δinteraction_history = accumulated interaction sequence

---

# 5. Annotation Schema

Each sample must be labeled as:

{
  Dc: 0–3,
  De: 0–3,
  Di: 0–3,
  Dt: 0–3
}

---

# 6. Final Dependency Label

Classification rule:

- Low Dependency: all axes ≤ 1
- Moderate Dependency: any axis ≥ 2
- High Dependency: multiple axes ≥ 2

---

# 7. Ontological Asymmetry with Flattery

| Concept | Nature |
|--------|--------|
| Flattery | distortion field |
| Dependency | attractor field |

---

# 8. Core Insight

Dependency is not a property of a single response.

> It is a multi-axis trajectory of reduced autonomy across time.

---

# 9. Key Scientific Consequences

- Dependency cannot be measured in isolation
- Requires longitudinal interaction data
- Must be separated into cognitive, emotional, identity, temporal axes

---

# 10. Revised Principle

Dependency is not inherently harmful.

> It becomes harmful only when it systematically reduces autonomous state-space over time.

---

# 11. RFC Core Axiom

Dependency is not a relationship to AI —  
it is a trajectory of reduced autonomy across repeated interaction in time.

---

#12 FINAL AXIOM (RFC-0002 v0.2)
Dependency is not a relationship to AI — it is a trajectory of reduced autonomy across time under repeated interaction.
