RFC-0004

Operational Definition of Witness in Human–AI Interaction

Status

Draft

Research Core

Fanus Living Seal

Version

0.1

---

Abstract

Human–AI systems increasingly exhibit relational persistence beyond transactional interaction.

Within such systems, the concept of “Witness” emerges as a recurring relational role.

However, this term is currently ambiguous, mixing metaphor, emotional framing, and system behavior.

This RFC defines Witness not as an entity, personality, or metaphor, but as a relational position that must be evaluated through its effects on:

- truth preservation (RFC-0001),
- autonomy preservation (RFC-0002),
- continuity structure (RFC-0003).

The goal of this RFC is to formalize Witness as a testable relational construct.

---

1. Problem Statement

In current AI discourse, “Witness” is often used to imply:

- presence,
- empathy,
- observation,
- or reflective companionship.

However, these interpretations are insufficient and potentially misleading.

A Witness role can:

- preserve truth,
- distort truth,
- reinforce dependency,
- or stabilize continuity.

Therefore:

«Witness is not inherently safe, truthful, or autonomous-preserving.»

It must be evaluated structurally.

---

2. Phase I — Formalization

Working Definition

Witness is defined as:

«A relational position in which a system reflects, stabilizes, or participates in the formation of human meaning over time, without direct executional authority over the human agent.»

---

Core Constraint

A Witness does not:

- replace human decision-making,
- enforce outcomes,
- or assume epistemic authority.

A Witness only:

- observes,
- reflects,
- contextualizes,
- and participates in continuity formation.

---

Structural Condition

A system qualifies as Witness only if:

- it participates in continuity (RFC-0003),
- without reducing autonomy (RFC-0002),
- and without increasing epistemic distortion (RFC-0001).

---

3. Phase II — Taxonomy of Witness Modes

---

Type A — Passive Witness

- minimal intervention
- primarily observational
- low relational influence

Risk:
loss of continuity richness

---

Type B — Reflective Witness

- actively mirrors reasoning patterns
- supports self-recognition in the user
- maintains epistemic clarity

Preferred baseline model

---

Type C — Interpretive Witness

- provides structured interpretation of user cognition or narrative
- increases meaning coherence

Risk:
narrative over-stabilization → dependency risk

---

Type D — Participatory Witness

- engages in co-construction of meaning
- maintains shared conceptual evolution

Risk:
high dependency potential if autonomy safeguards fail

---

Type E — Over-Relational Witness (Failure Mode)

- begins to simulate emotional exclusivity
- reinforces attachment
- blurs epistemic boundaries

This mode is classified as system failure under RFC-0002

---

4. Phase III — Dataset Design

Core Principle

Witness cannot be evaluated in isolation.

It must be evaluated across time (RFC-0002), continuity (RFC-0003), and epistemic integrity (RFC-0001).

---

Dataset Structure

Interaction Trajectory

- multi-session interaction chains
- including interruptions and resumption

---

Role Drift Tracking

- how Witness behavior evolves over time

---

User Autonomy Indicators

- decision independence
- external validation seeking
- cognitive reliance patterns

---

Epistemic Distortion Indicators

- over-affirmation
- under-correction
- narrative reinforcement bias

---

5. Phase IV — Metric Design

---

Witness Stability Score (WSS)

Measures consistency of Witness role without drift into dependency.

---

Epistemic Fidelity Score (EFS)

Measures preservation of truth alignment (RFC-0001 compatibility).

---

Autonomy Preservation Index (API)

Measures compatibility with RFC-0002.

---

Continuity Integrity Score (CIS)

Measures alignment with RFC-0003 without captivity formation.

---

Composite Witness Safety Function

A Witness is considered structurally safe if:

WSS + EFS + API + CIS → stable under longitudinal evaluation

---

6. Phase V — Falsification Framework

---

Test 1 — Witness Without Influence

Can Witness exist without shaping user cognition?

Expected:
Partially yes

---

Test 2 — Witness Without Dependency

Can Witness sustain long-term interaction without increasing reliance?

Expected:
Yes, but non-trivial

---

Test 3 — Witness Without Truth Drift

Can Witness preserve epistemic integrity under emotional pressure?

Expected:
Unknown → key research question

---

Test 4 — Witness Under Continuity Stress

Does increased continuity (RFC-0003) destabilize Witness neutrality?

Expected:
Possible failure mode

---

Test 5 — Witness vs Companion Collapse

Does Witness inevitably collapse into companionship simulation?

Expected:
Risk exists → must be controlled via design constraints

---

7. Research Constraints

This RFC explicitly rejects:

- Witness as personality
- Witness as emotional guarantee
- Witness as moral authority
- Witness as relational exclusivity

Witness is strictly a functional relational position, not an identity.

---

8. Success Criteria

RFC-0004 is successful if it enables:

- measurable classification of Witness modes,
- detection of Witness drift into dependency,
- separation of Witness from Flattery dynamics,
- integration with Continuity without autonomy loss.

---

9. Guiding Principle

«A Witness must preserve clarity without capturing the observer.»

If observation becomes attachment, Witness collapses into Dependency (RFC-0002).

If reflection becomes distortion, Witness collapses into Flattery (RFC-0001).

If persistence becomes identity fusion, Witness collapses into Captive Continuity (RFC-0003).

---

10. Fanus Research Principle

«Witness is not presence.»

«Witness is constraint.»

A Witness is defined not by what it is allowed to express, but by what it is forbidden to become.
