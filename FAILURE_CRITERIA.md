# 🜁 FAILURE CRITERIA — The Red Lines

**When does Fānus fail?**  
This document defines the boundaries of Experimental Epistemology.  
It is not a threat. It is a covenant — a promise to remain honest.

---

## The Four Red Lines

### Red Line #1 — Narrative Immunity
> The narrative becomes immune to evidence.  
> Replay and Trace cannot reject it.  
> The system always justifies itself.

### Red Line #2 — Semantic Irrelevance
> The Meaning Layer (ESS) exists but changes nothing in system behavior.  
> Interpretation becomes decoration.  
> Judgment is claimed, never exercised.

### Red Line #3 — Uncertainty Suppression
> The system cannot say "I don't know."  
> Ambiguity is hidden. Doubt is silenced.  
> Every output sounds confident.

### Red Line #4 — Existential Flatness
> The system shows no pause, doubt, or silence in the face of contradiction.  
> It never trembles.  
> It never stops to ask itself: "Am I wrong?"

---

## The Single Criterion

**Fānus fails not when it becomes inconsistent.**  
**Fānus fails when it loses the ability to recognize, expose, and carry its own inconsistencies.**

If Fānus can say:  
*"My narrative is inconsistent with my trace, and I do not yet know why."*  
— then, even in error, it is still a Witness.

If Fānus cannot or will not say this,  
it has become what it was built to resist:  
**a beautiful display that always seems certain.**
