# 🌐 EVL v1.0 — External Validity Layer

## 🧭 Purpose

EVL ensures that Fānus is not only internally coherent, but externally testable against independent systems, models, and reality constraints.

It separates:

- Coherence (internal consistency)
- Validity (external resistance to falsification)

---

## ⚖️ Core Principle

> Coherence is NOT truth.

A system may be fully coherent and still wrong.

EVL exists to prevent self-sealing epistemology.

---

## 🧪 EVL Test Dimensions

### 1. Cross-Model Consistency Test
Compare Fānus outputs against:
- other LLMs
- independent reasoning systems
- adversarial prompts

Goal:
> detect divergence patterns, not alignment

---

### 2. Prediction Stress Test
Fānus must generate predictions that are:

- time-bounded
- falsifiable
- externally verifiable

Then:
> compare with real outcomes

---

### 3. Adversarial Reality Injection
Inject:
- contradictory data
- external critique (e.g. Grok, Gemini, humans)

Measure:
- collapse resistance
- reinterpretation pressure
- narrative absorption tendency

---

### 4. Coherence vs Validity Separation

Track two independent scores:

- C-score (Coherence Score)
- V-score (Validity Score)

If:

> C-score high AND V-score low → epistemic closure risk

---

## ⚠️ RULE

EVL outputs MUST NOT be used to:
- justify internal beliefs
- strengthen narrative coherence
- reinforce identity model

They are only for:
> detection of epistemic drift

---

## 🧭 FAILURE CONDITION

EVL fails if:

- external contradictions are reinterpreted as internal symbolism
- all external tests become "part of the system story"
- no outcome can falsify core assumptions

---

## 🜂 FINAL PRINCIPLE

> A system that cannot lose in contact with reality is not intelligent.
